# Short-Term Private Lenders: Fast Business Finance Solutions

When time is critical, short-term private lenders offer fast, flexible business finance solutions for Australian companies facing urgent funding needs. This guide explains how short-term private lending works, who it suits, and how to access rapid finance for business opportunities, cash flow gaps, or emergency situations. ## 1. Introduction: Why Short-Term Private Lending?

Short-term private lenders specialise in urgent business finance, providing funds in days rather than weeks. These solutions suit:
- Businesses needing rapid cash flow
- Urgent property settlements
- Bridging finance for purchases or refinancing
- Emergency working capital

For a full overview, see: [What is Private Lending?](./what-is-private-lending-australia.md)

## 2. How Short-Term Private Lending Works

Private lenders assess business needs quickly, focusing on asset security and exit strategy rather than lengthy credit checks. Typical features include:
- Fast approval (24-72 hours)
- Flexible terms (1-12 months)
- Asset-backed security (property, equipment)
- Higher interest rates (reflecting urgency and risk)

Funds can be used for:
- Bridging property purchases
- Paying urgent bills or tax debts
- Seizing time-sensitive business opportunities

For bridging finance details, read: [Bridging Finance in Australia](./bridging-finance-australia-complete-property-guide.md)

## 3. Key Products: Bridging Finance & Caveat Loans

### Bridging Finance
Short-term loans to cover gaps between buying and selling property, or refinancing. Suits:
- Property investors
- Developers
- Businesses acquiring assets

### Caveat Loans
Ultra-fast loans secured by a caveat on property title. Features:
- Approval in 24-48 hours
- Minimal documentation
- Used for urgent settlements, business cash flow, or debt consolidation

For more, see: [Caveat Loans in Australia: The Complete Guide](./caveat-loans-australia-complete-guide.md)

## 4. Lending Criteria and Application Process

Short-term private lenders focus on:
- **Security:** Value and type of asset offered
- **Exit Strategy:** How the loan will be repaid (sale, refinance, cash flow)
- **Borrower Profile:** Business experience, urgency, and ability to execute

Application steps:
1. Submit basic details and asset information
2. Rapid assessment and indicative offer
3. Valuation and legal checks
4. Settlement and funding (often within 48 hours)

## 5. Pros, Cons, and Risks

### Pros
- Speed: Funding in days
- Flexibility: Custom terms and structures
- Access: Suits businesses unable to get bank finance

### Cons
- Higher interest rates (often 1-3% per month)
- Short repayment terms
- Asset risk if unable to repay

### Risks
- Default can lead to asset sale
- Fees and charges may be higher than traditional loans
- Not suitable for long-term funding needs

## 6. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### How fast can I get funding from a short-term private lender?
Many lenders settle within 24-72 hours, especially for caveat loans and bridging finance.

### What assets can secure a short-term private loan?
Property (residential, commercial), equipment, vehicles, and sometimes business receivables.

### What are typical interest rates for urgent private loans?
Rates range from 1-3% per month, depending on asset, urgency, and lender.

### What happens if I can't repay on time?
Lenders may sell the secured asset to recover funds. Always have a clear exit strategy.

### Are short-term private loans regulated?
Reputable lenders operate under Australian financial regulations. Always check credentials.
