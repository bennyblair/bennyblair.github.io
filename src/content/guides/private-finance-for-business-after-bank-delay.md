Private finance for business after a bank delay is short-term or alternative commercial funding considered when a mainstream lender cannot move quickly enough for a business deadline. It may help where the borrower still has a viable transaction, usable security, and a clear repayment or refinance plan, but it is not a shortcut around weak fundamentals.

A bank delay can create real pressure. Settlement dates, supplier deadlines, tax arrangements, acquisition contracts, fitout works, and refinance expiries often keep moving even when credit assessment slows down. The question is not simply whether private finance is available. The question is whether it solves the timing problem without creating a larger one.

## What Private Finance Means After a Bank Delay

Private finance is business-purpose funding provided by non-bank, private credit, or specialist lenders. After a bank delay, it is usually considered as a bridge, second mortgage, caveat loan, private mortgage, or short-term commercial facility that keeps a transaction moving while the bank process is resolved or replaced.

The best use case is a timing mismatch. The borrower has a real transaction, the bank may still be suitable in the long run, but the current process cannot settle by the required date. Private finance can sometimes provide a temporary structure where the security and exit are clear.

This is different from using private finance because the transaction cannot support debt. If the business has no repayment plan, no credible refinance path, or no viable commercial purpose, a private facility may only move the stress from today to a later deadline.

## When To Use Private Finance After a Delay

Private finance may fit where the bank delay is procedural rather than fundamental. Examples include valuation timing, internal credit backlog, slow document review, pending refinance approval, or a settlement deadline that arrives before a mainstream lender can complete.

It may also fit when the borrower has property security and a defined exit. That exit might be a bank refinance, property sale, incoming receivable, business sale, project completion, or another funding event. For property-backed files, a broker may compare [caveat loans](/resources/guides/caveat-loans-australia-complete-guide), [second mortgages without refinancing](/resources/guides/second-mortgage-without-refinancing-your-first-mortgage-in-australia), and [bridge lending for commercial property](/resources/guides/bridge-lending-australia-commercial-property).

A private lender may also be relevant where the bank's structure is too rigid for the commercial reality. For example, the business may have strong asset backing but uneven trading results, or the transaction may involve a time-sensitive acquisition where a bank cannot complete all checks before the contract deadline.

## When Not To Use Private Finance

Private finance is usually not suitable where the borrower has no exit strategy. If the only plan is to refinance later without evidence that a lender is likely to support the refinance, the short-term facility may create a maturity problem.

It may also be unsuitable where the requested amount is too high for the available security, where the borrower cannot explain the use of funds, or where bank delays are caused by serious unresolved issues such as legal disputes, unclear ownership, tax arrears with no plan, or incomplete financial records.

For ongoing operating pressure, consider whether the business needs restructuring, [business debt consolidation](/resources/guides/business-debt-consolidation-australia), [invoice finance](/resources/guides/invoice-finance-australia-complete-guide), or a more permanent working capital facility instead of a short-term private loan.

## What Lenders Want To Understand

Private lenders usually want a concise explanation of the delay. Was the bank waiting on a valuation? Did credit ask for more information? Was the bank approval withdrawn, or is it simply not ready? The answer changes the way the private lender sees risk.

They also focus on security and exit. Security may include commercial property, residential investment property used for business-purpose borrowing, business assets, receivables, equipment, or another acceptable support. The exit may be a refinance, sale, repayment from business funds, or another documented event.

A strong file does not hide the bank delay. It explains it plainly, supports the transaction with evidence, and shows why the private facility is a bridge to a credible outcome rather than a last-minute rescue with no plan.

## Documents That Improve the File

Prepare a one-page transaction summary before approaching lenders. It should cover the borrower, business purpose, amount required, deadline, security offered, current bank status, and proposed exit.

Useful supporting documents include bank approval correspondence, valuation updates, contracts, lease schedules, payout figures, company documents, identification, recent financials, BAS where relevant, ATO position if tax timing is involved, and solicitor or accountant contacts.

For commercial property transactions, include title details, current debt, settlement statements, lease information, and purchase or sale contracts. If the issue is a stalled refinance, the guide on [commercial loan refinance](/resources/guides/commercial-loan-refinance) may help frame the file.

## How To Compare Private Finance With Waiting for the Bank

The first comparison is deadline risk. If waiting for the bank means losing a contract, missing settlement, breaching a payment arrangement, or stopping a project, private finance may be commercially worth assessing.

The second comparison is total cost. Private finance can cost more than bank finance, so the borrower should compare the total expected cost of the short-term facility against the cost of delay, default, lost opportunity, or contract failure. Avoid focusing only on speed.

The third comparison is exit certainty. If the bank is likely to complete in a short period, a private bridge may make sense. If the bank has declined because the file does not meet policy and no alternative exit exists, the private facility needs much closer scrutiny.

## Risk Controls Before Signing

Ask for all fees, legal costs, default costs, minimum term requirements, extension conditions, and discharge costs in writing. The commercial decision should be based on total cost, not only the rate or headline approval speed.

Build a realistic timeline. If the bank says a refinance may take several weeks, do not structure a private facility around a best-case date with no buffer. If a property sale is the exit, consider contract conditions, settlement risk, and what happens if the buyer delays.

Use professional advice. Accountants, solicitors, and commercial finance specialists can help test whether the facility solves the real problem and whether the exit plan is credible.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is private finance for business after a bank delay?
Private finance for business after a bank delay is alternative commercial funding considered when a bank process is too slow for a business deadline. It is usually used as a short-term bridge to a refinance, sale, settlement, or other repayment event.

### Is private finance a replacement for bank finance?
Private finance can replace bank finance in some commercial scenarios, but after a bank delay it is often used as a temporary bridge. The borrower should understand the purpose, cost, term, and exit strategy before committing.

### When does private finance make sense after a bank delay?
Private finance may make sense where the delay is procedural, the transaction is still viable, the borrower has suitable security, and the expected repayment or refinance event is credible. It is strongest when it solves a timing problem.

### What documents do private lenders need?
Private lenders usually need a clear transaction summary, identification, company documents, security details, current debt information, bank correspondence, contracts or settlement documents, and evidence supporting the proposed exit strategy.

### What is the main risk of using private finance after a delay?
The main risk is replacing a bank timing problem with a short-term loan that cannot be repaid or refinanced. If the exit does not happen, the borrower may face extra costs, maturity pressure, or enforcement risk.

### Can private finance help if a bank has declined the application?
Private finance may help after a bank decline if the issue is policy fit, timing, or documentation and the borrower still has suitable security and a credible exit. It should not be treated as guaranteed approval or a way to avoid unresolved business problems.
