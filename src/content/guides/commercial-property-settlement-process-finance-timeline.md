# Commercial Property Settlement Process: Finance Timeline

Commercial property settlement involves coordinated execution between purchasers, vendors, financiers, legal representatives, and various service providers. Unlike residential transactions, commercial settlements feature greater complexity, extended timeframes, conditional requirements, and multi-party coordination.

This guide outlines the commercial property settlement timeline from contract exchange through to post-settlement completion, highlighting finance coordination milestones, documentation requirements, common challenges, and strategies for managing timing pressures in Australian business property transactions.

## At a Glance

- **Typical settlement periods**: 30-90 days for straightforward transactions; 90-180+ days for complex deals
- **Finance approval timing**: 4-8 weeks for standard commercial loans; 2-4 weeks for pre-approved facilities
- **Critical path items**: Finance approval, due diligence completion, documentation execution, funds clearance
- **Common delays**: Finance conditions, valuation issues, tenant documentation, development approvals
- **Coordination requirements**: Legal, financial, valuation, building, environmental, and planning aspects
- **Risk management**: Finance contingency periods, extension mechanisms, sunset date planning

## Standard Commercial Settlement Timeline

### Phase 1: Pre-Contract (Days -14 to 0)
**Activities before contract exchange establishing transaction foundation**

| Day | Activity | Description | Finance Implications |
|-----|----------|-------------|----------------------|
| -14 to -7 | Initial due diligence | Property inspection, preliminary title review, zoning checks | Informs finance feasibility and lender requirements |
| -7 to -3 | Finance pre-assessment | Initial lender discussions, indicative terms, application preparation | Establishes borrowing capacity and likely conditions |
| -3 to -1 | Contract review | Solicitor examines contract, identifies special conditions | Finance clauses (subject to finance) critical for protection |
| 0 | Contract exchange | Contracts signed, deposit paid (typically 5-10%) | Finance clock starts; due diligence period commences |

**Key Finance Considerations**:
- Include "subject to finance" clause with reasonable period (21-42 days)
- Specify deposit holding arrangements (stakeholder vs vendor)
- Document any vendor finance or transitional arrangements
- Clarify conditions precedent affecting settlement timing

### Phase 2: Finance Application & Due Diligence (Days 1-28)
**Critical period for securing finance and completing investigations**

| Day | Activity | Description | Finance Implications |
|-----|----------|-------------|----------------------|
| 1-3 | Formal finance application | Submit complete application to chosen lender(s) | Documentation quality affects approval speed |
| 4-10 | Lender assessment | Credit analysis, preliminary property review | Respond promptly to information requests |
| 7-14 | Property valuation | Lender-ordered valuation conducted | Valuation outcome critical for loan approval |
| 10-21 | Due diligence completion | Building inspections, environmental reports, tenant reviews | Findings may trigger finance renegotiation |
| 14-28 | Formal approval issued | Lender provides loan offer with conditions | Conditions must be satisfied before settlement |
| 21-28 | Documentation preparation | Mortgage documents, security agreements drafted | Legal review period required |

**Key Finance Considerations**:
- Maintain communication with lender throughout assessment
- Address valuation issues promptly (may require additional equity or guarantor)
- Satisfy approval conditions methodically with documentation evidence
- Coordinate legal representatives between purchaser and lender

### Phase 3: Pre-Settlement Preparation (Days 29-42)
**Final preparations before settlement date**

| Day | Activity | Description | Finance Implications |
|-----|----------|-------------|----------------------|
| 29-35 | Documentation execution | Sign mortgage documents, security agreements | Ensure signing authorities correct for entity type |
| 32-38 | Funds arrangement | Confirm equity available, coordinate drawdown | Lender may require funds 1-2 days before settlement |
| 35-40 | Pre-settlement inspection | Final property inspection, tenant confirmation | Verify property condition matches contract |
| 38-42 | Settlement statement review | Solicitor reviews adjustment calculations | Confirm rates, outgoings, rent adjustments accurate |
| 40-42 | Final lender checks | Lender confirms all conditions satisfied | No outstanding requirements before funds release |

**Key Finance Considerations**:
- Ensure equity funds cleared in relevant account before settlement
- Confirm insurance arrangements effective from settlement
- Verify tenant documentation complete if tenanted property
- Address any last-minute title issues or encumbrances

### Phase 4: Settlement Day (Day 43)
**Transaction completion and property transfer**

| Time | Activity | Description | Finance Implications |
|------|----------|-------------|----------------------|
| 9:00 AM | Final checks | All parties confirm readiness for settlement | Lender authorizes funds transfer |
| 10:00 AM | Electronic settlement (PEXA) | Digital settlement platform transaction | Funds transferred, titles updated electronically |
| 10:30 AM | Settlement confirmation | All parties receive settlement confirmation | Property legally transfers to purchaser |
| 11:00 AM | Post-settlement tasks | Keys released, tenant notifications, insurance activation | Lender registers mortgage on title |
| 12:00 PM | Completion | Transaction complete, possession available | Loan repayments commence per agreement |

**Key Finance Considerations**:
- PEXA requires pre-authorization and digital certificate setup
- Lender funds typically released morning of settlement
- Settlement may occur earlier if all parties ready
- Post-settlement registration occurs automatically through PEXA

### Phase 5: Post-Settlement (Days 44-60)
**Post-completion activities and transition**

| Day | Activity | Description | Finance Implications |
|-----|----------|-------------|----------------------|
| 44-46 | Documentation distribution | Final documents to all parties, title copies | Mortgage documentation filed appropriately |
| 47-52 | Tenant management | Notify tenants of ownership change, update records | Rental payments may redirect to new owner |
| 50-56 | Accounting integration | Property records added to business accounts | Loan liability recorded, depreciation schedules prepared |
| 55-60 | Lender follow-up | Confirm registration complete, ongoing requirements | Compliance with loan covenants commences |

## Extended Timelines: Complex Transactions

### Development Site Acquisition (90-180 days)
**Additional considerations affecting settlement timing**:

- **Planning approval conditions**: Settlement may be subject to development approval
- **Contamination reports**: Environmental assessments may extend due diligence
- **Infrastructure agreements**: Council requirements for developer contributions
- **Staged settlements**: Partial settlements for large or phased acquisitions
- **Finance complexity**: Development finance requires additional assessment

### Tenanted Investment Property (60-120 days)
**Additional considerations affecting settlement timing**:

- **Lease documentation review**: Examination of all tenant leases and agreements
- **Rental verification**: Confirmation of income, outgoings, and lease terms
- **Tenant estoppel certificates**: Formal tenant confirmation of lease status
- **Assignment consents**: Landlord consent requirements for lease transfers
- **Security bond transfers**: Re-registration of tenant bonds to new owner

### Business Acquisition with Property (75-150 days)
**Additional considerations affecting settlement timing**:

- **Business due diligence**: Financial records, contracts, employee arrangements
- **Goodwill valuation**: Separation of property value from business value
- **Licence transfers**: Business licences, permits, and regulatory approvals
- **Stock and asset valuation**: Inventory assessment and transfer arrangements
- **Vendor transition**: Handover periods and training requirements

## Finance-Specific Timeline Considerations

### Bank vs Non-Bank Lender Timelines

| Lender Type | Typical Approval Time | Settlement Flexibility | Common Conditions |
|-------------|----------------------|-----------------------|-------------------|
| **Major Banks** | 6-8 weeks | Limited (rigid processes) | Extensive financials, valuations, internal approvals |
| **Regional Banks** | 4-6 weeks | Moderate | Strong focus on relationship and local market knowledge |
| **Non-Bank Lenders** | 3-5 weeks | High | Property-focused, faster decision-making |
| **Private Lenders** | 2-4 weeks | Very high | Equity-driven, streamlined documentation |

### Pre-Approval Advantages
Securing finance pre-approval before property search provides timeline benefits:

1. **Reduced conditional period**: Finance essentially approved subject to property
2. **Stronger negotiation position**: Demonstrates purchaser capacity and seriousness
3. **Faster settlement possible**: Can often settle within 21-30 days of contract
4. **Certainty in competitive situations**: Ability to offer shorter settlements

### Bridging Finance Timeline Considerations
When using bridging finance for settlement timing gaps:

- **Approval speed**: 5-10 business days typical for straightforward bridging
- **Documentation**: Streamlined compared to long-term finance
- **Exit strategy focus**: Critical component of assessment
- **Coordination**: Must align with primary finance settlement timing

## Common Settlement Delays and Solutions

### 1. Finance Approval Delays
**Causes**: Incomplete documentation, valuation issues, credit assessment queries, internal lender processes
**Solutions**: 
- Submit complete application with all required documents
- Engage mortgage broker to manage lender communication
- Consider alternative lenders if primary option slow
- Build contingency period into contract (42+ days)

### 2. Valuation Issues
**Causes**: Property not meeting lender expectations, comparable evidence limited, specialized property type
**Solutions**:
- Provide comprehensive property information to valuer
- Consider multiple valuation providers if first unsatisfactory
- Negotiate with lender on alternative security or reduced LVR
- Seek specialist lender familiar with property type

### 3. Due Diligence Findings
**Causes**: Building defects, environmental concerns, planning restrictions, tenant issues
**Solutions**:
- Conduct thorough due diligence early in process
- Negotiate price adjustment or vendor rectification
- Consider specialist insurance (defects, environmental)
- Renegotiate finance based on updated risk assessment

### 4. Documentation Complexities
**Causes**: Complex entity structures, multiple security properties, cross-collateralization
**Solutions**:
- Engage experienced commercial property solicitor early
- Simplify structure where possible for initial transaction
- Allow additional time for lender legal review
- Consider staged documentation approach

### 5. Tenant-Related Delays
**Causes**: Incomplete lease documentation, tenant estoppel delays, assignment consent issues
**Solutions**:
- Review tenant documentation during due diligence period
- Engage property manager to coordinate tenant communications
- Include specific tenant condition timelines in contract
- Consider vacant possession if timing critical

## Critical Path Management Strategies

### 1. Parallel Processing
Execute multiple activities simultaneously rather than sequentially:
- Finance application while conducting due diligence
- Valuation ordered while lender assesses application
- Documentation preparation while awaiting formal approval

### 2. Contingency Planning
Build realistic buffers into timeline:
- Add 7-14 days to estimated finance approval period
- Include extension mechanisms in contract (mutual agreement)
- Identify alternative finance options if primary falls through
- Plan for potential valuation or due diligence issues

### 3. Professional Coordination
Engage integrated professional team:
- Solicitor experienced in commercial property transactions
- Mortgage broker specializing in commercial finance
- Accountant familiar with property acquisition structures
- Property manager for tenanted investments

### 4. Communication Protocol
Establish clear communication channels:
- Regular update schedule between all parties
- Designated primary contact for each professional service
- Document tracking system for conditions and requirements
- Escalation process for emerging issues

## Post-Settlement Finance Management

### Immediate Post-Settlement (First 30 days)
1. **Loan account setup**: Confirm direct debit arrangements, online access
2. **Insurance verification**: Ensure policies effective, lender noted as interested party
3. **Property management transition**: Update records, redirect rental income if applicable
4. **Accounting integration**: Record asset, liability, and depreciation schedules

### Ongoing Compliance (Months 1-12)
1. **Financial reporting**: Provide periodic financials if required by lender
2. **Insurance renewals**: Maintain adequate coverage with lender notification
3. **Property performance**: Monitor rental income, vacancies, maintenance
4. **Covenant compliance**: Adhere to loan terms including LVR maintenance

### Refinance Preparation (6-12 months before expiry)
1. **Market monitoring**: Track interest rates, lender offerings
2. **Property performance**: Maintain or enhance income and value
3. **Documentation readiness**: Keep financial records current
4. **Early engagement**: Discuss options with broker 6+ months before maturity

## Special Considerations by Property Type

### Office Buildings
- **Tenant mix review**: Credit quality of tenants affects finance assessment
- **Lease expiry profile**: Concentration risk in lease expiries
- **Building quality**: NABERS ratings, sustainability features
- **Location attributes**: Proximity to transport, amenities, competing supply

### Industrial Properties
- **Clear height and loading**: Functional specifications affecting value
- **Land component**: Land-to-asset ratio influencing depreciation
- **Tenant suitability**: Specialized improvements for specific industries
- **Transport access**: Proximity to highways, ports, rail networks

### Retail Properties
- **Trading history**: Retail sales evidence if owner-occupied
- **Tenant covenants**: National vs independent tenant security
- **Centre positioning**: Within larger retail complex or standalone
- **Car parking**: Adequacy for catchment and trading patterns

### Development Sites
- **Planning certainty**: Level of approval and remaining conditions
- **Infrastructure capacity**: Services availability and upgrade requirements
- **Holding costs**: Interest capitalisation during development period
- **Presale requirements**: Percentage needed for construction finance
