# Commercial Loan Maturity Refinance: What to Do Before the Facility Expires

A commercial loan maturity refinance is the process of replacing, extending, or restructuring a commercial facility before its expiry date. For Australian business owners, property investors, and developers, the maturity date matters because the lender may expect full repayment, a new credit approval, or a formal refinance pathway rather than simply rolling the loan forward.

The safest time to deal with a maturing commercial loan is before the lender's deadline becomes urgent. A file that starts 90 to 180 days before expiry usually has more room for valuations, financial updates, lease reviews, credit questions, and fallback options than a file raised in the final week.

This guide explains how commercial loan maturity refinance works, what lenders check, when private credit or bridging finance may help, and when finance is not a substitute for accounting, legal, or insolvency advice. It is general information only and not financial advice.

## When To Use a Maturity Refinance Strategy

Use a maturity refinance strategy when the loan expiry date is known and the borrower needs a credible repayment or renewal pathway. The goal is not just to find a replacement lender. The goal is to present a file that explains the asset, borrower, conduct, numbers, risks, and exit clearly enough for a lender to make a decision.

A strategy is especially useful when the asset has changed since the original approval. Examples include a tenant vacancy, lease expiry, lower valuation, construction delay, covenant breach, tax arrears, or a business downturn. These issues do not automatically make refinance impossible, but they need to be addressed directly.

## When Not To Use Finance as the First Answer

Do not treat refinance as the first answer if the business is insolvent, unable to meet statutory obligations, or facing legal demands that require specialist advice. In those cases, speak with an accountant, solicitor, or insolvency adviser before taking on more debt.

Finance also may not be suitable if there is no realistic repayment plan. A short-term private credit bridge can create breathing room, but it can also make the position worse if there is no sale, refinance, equity injection, or trading recovery behind it.

## Citation-Ready Answer: What Happens When a Commercial Loan Matures?

When a commercial loan matures, the existing facility reaches its agreed expiry date and the lender may require repayment, renewal approval, or formal refinance. Unlike some consumer-style facilities, commercial loans are commonly written for defined terms, so borrowers should not assume the loan will roll over automatically. A maturing commercial loan should be reviewed well before expiry because valuation changes, lease income, borrower financials, lender appetite, tax arrears, and conduct history can all affect renewal or refinance. ## The 180-Day Refinance Timeline

A practical commercial loan maturity plan starts around six months before expiry. At this point, the borrower should confirm the maturity date, current balance, security position, lease details, financials, tax status, and any lender conditions attached to renewal.

By 120 days out, the file should be lender-ready. That usually means updated financial statements, management accounts, rental schedules, lease documents, rates notices, mortgage statements, trust or company documents, and a clear explanation of the refinance purpose. If the property is specialised or regional, valuation evidence may need more time.

By 90 days out, the borrower should know whether the existing lender is likely to renew. If the answer is uncertain, a fallback path should already be moving. The fallback may be a [commercial property refinance after a bank decline](/resources/guides/commercial-property-refinance-after-a-bank-decline-in-australia), a private credit refinance, a partial repayment, or a sale plan.

## What Lenders Check Before Refinancing a Maturing Commercial Loan

Lenders first check whether the property or business still supports the debt. For commercial property, that includes valuation, lease strength, vacancy risk, tenant quality, zoning, location, and marketability. For business-purpose facilities, lenders also review trading performance, cash flow, tax position, debt conduct, and director background.

The most common friction point is valuation. If the new valuation is lower than expected, the borrower may need to reduce debt, add security, accept a different lender, or use a short-term structure while improving the file. The guide to [commercial property valuation disputes](/resources/guides/commercial-property-valuation-dispute-finance-options-australia) explains what to do when a conservative valuation changes the refinance equation.

Lenders also want to understand why the facility is maturing without a simple bank renewal. A clean explanation helps. A vague answer creates risk because it suggests the borrower has not understood the problem.

| Financials are delayed | The file is harder to approve | Provide management accounts, BAS, bank statements, and a document timetable. |

## Bank Renewal, Non-Bank Refinance, or Private Credit Bridge?

A bank renewal may be the lowest-friction option if the borrower still meets policy and the lender wants the exposure. It usually requires updated documents and a fresh assessment. If the bank is comfortable, this can avoid unnecessary refinance costs.

A non-bank refinance may suit borrowers who are still commercially viable but do not fit bank timing, documentation, security, or policy requirements. Non-bank lenders may take a more practical view of near-term lease issues, complex ownership, or temporary cash-flow pressure, but they still need a coherent repayment plan.

A private credit bridge may suit a compressed maturity deadline where the borrower needs time to complete a sale, refinance, lease renewal, or debt restructure. ## Documents To Prepare Before the Facility Expires

A stronger refinance file usually includes the current loan statement, payout figure, facility letter, security schedule, title details, company and trust documents, financial statements, management accounts, BAS, bank statements, tax status, lease documents, rental schedule, rates notice, insurance details, and any valuation or sales evidence.

The borrower should also prepare a short explanation of the facility history. What was the original purpose? How has the loan performed? Why is it maturing now? What changed? What is the requested structure? How will the new facility be repaid or refinanced?

A broker can help package this narrative so lenders are not forced to infer the story from disconnected documents. That matters because commercial credit teams are more comfortable when the risk is visible and explained.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is a commercial loan maturity refinance?

A commercial loan maturity refinance replaces, renews, or restructures a commercial facility before its expiry date. It is used when the existing loan is ending and the borrower needs a new approval, new lender, extension, sale bridge, or repayment plan.

### How early should I start refinancing a maturing commercial loan?

A practical starting point is 90 to 180 days before the facility expires. Complex properties, weak financials, tax arrears, lease expiry, or valuation uncertainty usually require more time because lenders may need extra evidence before approving a refinance.

### Will my bank automatically renew a commercial loan at maturity?

Commercial borrowers should not assume automatic renewal. Many lenders require a fresh credit assessment, updated financials, valuation review, lease review, and confirmation that the borrower still meets policy before extending or renewing a facility.

### Can private credit help if my commercial loan expires soon?

Private credit may help where the borrower has usable security, a clear commercial purpose, and a credible exit strategy, but timing alone is not enough. Lenders still assess valuation, leverage, documents, borrower conduct, and repayment pathway.

### What if the valuation is too low for refinance?

A lower valuation may require debt reduction, extra security, a different lender, a staged refinance, or a sale plan. The right response depends on the shortfall size, asset quality, existing debt, lease income, and borrower exit strategy.

### Is a maturity refinance the same as a commercial property refinance?

A maturity refinance is a type of commercial refinance triggered by a facility expiry date. A commercial property refinance can also happen for other reasons, such as seeking different terms, releasing equity, consolidating debt, or responding to a bank decline.
